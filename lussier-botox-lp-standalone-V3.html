Questions, Answered

The real questions about aesthetic treatments.

How do I know which treatment is right for me?
A consultation. We'll listen to your goals, assess what's actually driving them, and recommend a single treatment or a combination — whether that's softening expression lines, restoring volume, improving texture, or something else. There's no pressure to add on, and we'll quote your full plan up front.
How much do treatments cost?
Pricing varies by treatment. Wrinkle relaxers are priced per unit; fillers per syringe; skin treatments per session or as packages. We quote your full plan during your consultation, before you commit to anything. Ask about our Beauty Pre-Pay savings program for returning patients.
Do treatments hurt?
Most are very well-tolerated. Injectables involve brief pinches with ultra-fine needles. Skin resurfacing treatments use topical numbing — most patients describe them as warm rather than painful. We'll walk you through what to expect for whatever treatment you choose.
How much downtime is there?
It depends on the treatment. Most injectables have no real downtime — mild redness or swelling for a few hours. Skin resurfacing (Morpheus8, BBL/MOXI, peels) can mean 24–72 hours of redness, peeling, or sensitivity. We schedule everything around your calendar.
Who actually performs the treatments?
Treatments are performed by experienced registered nurses (Claire Bennett, RN and Jade Gonzales, RN, BSN) under the medical direction of Marc Lussier, MD, FACS — not technicians, not estheticians. Every protocol is reviewed by Dr. Lussier.
When will I see results, and how long do they last?
Wrinkle relaxers settle in 7–14 days and last 3–4 months. Fillers are visible immediately, settle in 2 weeks, and last 6–18 months depending on product and area. Skin resurfacing builds gradually over 4–12 weeks as collagen rebuilds.
Will I look "done"?
Not when treatment is calibrated correctly. The "overdone" look comes from doing too much, too fast — not from the treatments themselves. Our approach is conservative by default; we'd rather you come back for a touch-up than walk out unhappy.
